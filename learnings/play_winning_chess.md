# The Four Principles

Make decisions based of the four principles - force, time, space and pawn structure

## 1. Force

Force = strength of the pieces.

2 kinds of advantages in force
- More pieces overall
- More pieces in a particular area of the board

Piece Value - Pawn (1), Bishop/Knight (3), Rook (5), Queen (9)

Basic Tactics
- The Pin
- The Fork

Laying Traps
- types - those aimed at weak pawns, at undefended pieces and at the King's bodyguards.

  - A Pair of Weaklings - based on the weakness of the f7 and f2 pawns. ( exploited in Scholar's & Legall's Mate)
  - The Case of the Undefended Piece - Discovery/Discovered attack on undefended pieces.
  - The Battering Ram - dismantling the enemy king's cover ( playing for a devastating check )

King becomes an active piece in the endgame

## 2. Time

Advantage in time/tempo = Situation where you can bring your pieces to a particular part of the board faster than your opponent.
In many ways, concepts of time and force are similar.

Development is one key to a time advantage
- Don't move a piece twice in the opening

Going for a Gambit
- Voluntary sacrifice in the opening with the idea of a lead in development

Time advantage is temporary. When having it, try to make use of the superior force at your disposal.

## 3. Space

Squeezing an opponent to death -> one must first acquire a significant advantage in space.
Advantage in space = control more enemy territory than your opponent.

Space counting -> based on number of squares each piece controls in your enemy's territory.

Making use of space advantage
- Rooks - Rooks need open files to be effective.
- Bishops - Bishops neen open diagonals to reach their full potential. Fianchettoed bishop - bishop placed on the g7/b7 sqaure(for white).
- Knights - Knights are powerful as advanced scouts (ie- they need to be moved to an advanced square to reach its potential).

Defending against a space advantage
- Player with less space should try to trade some pieces ( & if you do have a space advantage, avoid trading pieces as much as possible).

## 4. Pawn structure

Weak pawns
- Pawn islands - seperate from other pawns by atleast one file. In the endgame, the one with most pawn islands have a disadvantage.
- Doubled pawns - are usually considered weak due to the lack of mobility as normal pawns. Can be useful on center files as they control key squares. It is usually not a liability if the front pawn is defended by a pawn.
- Tripled pawns - are always an out-and-out liability. Tend to be undefended by other pawns and are sitting ducks for enemy attack.
- Isolated pawns - is a pawn that has no friendly pawns on the files immediately next to it. They are considered weak as it has no protecting pawns on either side to give it support & must be constantly guarded by pieces.
- Backward pawns - are those which are behind neighbouring pawns and can't move forward as it is controlled by enemy piece/blocked by a piece.

Curing weak pawns
- Trade the weak pawn.
- Move it to where it is not vulnerable. ( while advancing pawns, make sure it is trailed by one pawn ).

Attacking weak pawns
- Maintain attack and win the pawn.
- gain control of the square in front of the pawn. ("before you attack a weak pawn, gain control of the square in front of it")

Good pawns - any pawn that exerts pressure on the opponent and is safe from attack.
- Passed pawns - no enemy pawns on either side of it (above it, direct left-right & behind not counted). Opponent needs to set roadblock to prevent advance.
- Protected passed pawns - A passed pawn which is also protected.

Pawn chain
- is neither good or bad. Is simply a group of pawns lined by on a diagonal.
- One must aim to attack the base(or potential base) of the chain.

Open & Closed Positions
- Open
  - min number of pawns at the center
  - pieces lead the attack (aim to get pieces out, king castled, rooks on open files)
  - bishops better than knights

- Closed
  - center filled with pawns
  - pawns lead the attack ( attack where there is more space to maneuver, king castling can be delayed a bit, create open files for rooks )
  - knights better than bishops
