# The Four Principles

Make decisions based of the four principles - force, time, space and pawn structure

## 1. Force

Force = strength of the pieces.

2 kinds of advantages in force
- More pieces overall
- More pieces in a particular area of the board

Piece Value - Pawn (1), Bishop/Knight (3), Rook (5), Queen (9)

Basic Tactics
- The Pin
- The Fork

Laying Traps
- types - those aimed at weak pawns, at undefended pieces and at the King's bodyguards.

  - A Pair of Weaklings - based on the weakness of the f7 and f2 pawns. ( exploited in Scholar's & Legall's Mate)
  - The Case of the Undefended Piece - Discovery/Discovered attack on undefended pieces.
  - The Battering Ram - dismantling the enemy king's cover ( playing for a devastating check )

King becomes an active piece in the endgame

## 2. Time

Advantage in time/tempo = Situation where you can bring your pieces to a particular part of the board faster than your opponent.
In many ways, concepts of time and force are similar.

Development is one key to a time advantage
- Don't move a piece twice in the opening

Going for a Gambit
- Voluntary sacrifice in the opening with the idea of a lead in development

Time advantage is temporary. When having it, try to make use of the superior force at your disposal.
